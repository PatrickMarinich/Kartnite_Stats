Infromation Page

Kartnite Versus Raiting (KVR)
  • Inspired by the Chess ELO rating scale and the MKWii VR system.
  • The KVR number represents a probablity that a player will beat another given player in a race.
  • The probablity player A beats player B is defined by: 0.5 + (0.5 * (1-e(-1/2500)*(Player A KVR - Player B KVR)))
  • This is approximatly a 10 percent change every 500 points.
  • As a reference point the COMs are at 5000 KVR with a small variace every given race (+- 250 points)
  • To calculate the KVR change after a race, a players expected placement is calculated using the their KVR compared to each other player
  • Then if a player gets a placement higher then expected, KVR will go up by the difference (times a scale factor of 20) and vice versa if they do worse then the expected placement.
Track MVP
  • Track MVP determines who is the best player on each track either seasonally or all-time
  • For Seasonal: It is determined by (0.96)*(Player Track Average) + 0.04(Total Track Points)
  • For All-Time: It is strictly determined by Player Track Average, however there is a minimum race count of 5
  • The reason is it calculated slightly differently is to ensure fairness in both the short term and long term
  • For Seasonal: It would be unfair if one player went 1/1 and another went 14/15, as due to sample size, the 14/15 result is much better, thus having a small percentage of total score count solves this problem
  • For Seasonal: A race minimum would not work as well here due to the asymmetry of race count among players and tracks in a short time frame
  • For All-Time: Having a race minimum accomplishes this due to it being long term with a large sample size
Kart Score
  • Kart Score is a seasonal stat which takes into account six different catigories
  • The catigories are: GP Wins, Track MVP Points, Race Points, Track Average Points, Shock Dodge Points, and Blue Shell Points
  • These are the points given out as it currently stands:
    • Each GP Point Scored: 1
    • Each GP Win: 100
    • Each Time A Track you are MVP of is Played: 0.5 Points Per Player
    • Each Shock Dodge: 5
    • Each Blue Shell: 0.5
    • Each Blue Dodge: 10
    • Average Score: 1 Point Per Average
  • There is also Normalized Kart Score for the All Time Stats
  • This is calculated by having your seasonal kart score converted into a percentage of the total kart score earned this current season
  • Then this is added to the previous seasonal scores decermented by 15 percent for each season that is has passed.
  • For example if it was season 4 the normalized kart score would be: This Seasons Score + 0.85(Last Season) + .7225(2 Seasons Ago) + 0.61(3 Seasons Ago)
Kart Rating
  • A stat inspired by QBR from football
  • A maximum Kart Raiting is 160 points
  • Up to 60 points are given from GP Win Percentage
  • Up to 60 Points are given from average placement
  • Up to 40 Points are given from Track MVPs
  • It is calculated by: 60(GP Win percentage) + 60(avg/15) + Track MVPs
Miscellaneous Points
  • Misc points is a stat whose goal is to keep track of the little things that happen each race
  • It takes into account Blue Shells, Blue Dodges, and Shock Dodges
  • It is calculated by:
    • Each Blue Shell: 1
    • Each Blue Shell Dodge: 10
    • Each Shock Dodge: 4
    • Then this sum is divided by the GPs played so it acts as an average per GP
Seeding Power Points
  • The goal of this stat is to combine Kart Score, Kart Rating, and Misc Points into a singular metric.
  • The idea being that each of these individual stats have their flaws, but by combining them, they cover the flaws of one another
  • Seeding Power Points is calccuated by looking at a players location on the leaderboard for each of these stats and summing them up
  • 4 Points are given for each position on the Kart Rating Leaderboard
  • 3 Points are given for each position on the Normalized Kart Score Leaderboard
